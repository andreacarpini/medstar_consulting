Questions? Answers.

Does my US health insurance cover medical treatment in Turkey?

Typically, no. Most standard US health insurance plans (like employer-provided insurance or Medicare) do not cover elective procedures performed overseas. Their networks are based in the United States. However, your plan may provide some coverage for unexpected emergencies that occur while you are already traveling. We strongly advise all our clients to purchase specialized Medical Travel Insurance to protect themselves against complications and to cover the cost of emergency medical evacuation, which we can help you arrange.

How do I know the doctors and hospitals in Turkey are safe and reputable?

Your safety is our top priority. We exclusively partner with hospitals that are JCI (Joint Commission International) accredited—the global gold standard for healthcare quality and patient safety. The surgeons and physicians we work with are vetted based on their international training (often in the US or EU), credentials, patient outcomes, and peer reputation. We will provide you with detailed profiles, success rates, and before/after galleries so you can make a confident, informed decision.

What about communication? Will there be a language barrier?

Absolutely not. Every physician and surgeon we work with is fluent in English. Furthermore, your dedicated patient coordinator will be with you every step of the way—from airport pickup to all your medical appointments—to ensure seamless communication. All medical documentation provided to you will be in English.

How far in advance do I need to plan my medical trip?

While Turkey offers much faster scheduling than the US, we recommend starting the process at least 4-8 weeks in advance. This allows sufficient time for: A thorough review of your medical records by the Turkish medical team. Creating a customized treatment plan and firm cost estimate. Securing travel documents (passport, e-Visa). Booking flights and accommodation at the best rates. For complex procedures, more lead time may be beneficial.

Can a family member or friend come with me?

Yes, and we encourage it. Having a companion provides invaluable emotional support. While the companion's flights are typically their own responsibility, the on-the-ground costs are often included or available at a highly discounted rate.

How does the payment process work?

For your security and transparency, you pay the hospital directly. We never handle your treatment funds. The hospital will typically require a deposit to secure your surgery date, with the balance due upon your arrival or before the procedure.

Do I need a visa to travel to Turkey for surgery?

US citizens need an e-Visa to enter Turkey, which is easily obtained online in just a few minutes for a small fee. It is a simple process for tourism purposes. For longer stays related to medical treatment, we will guide you on any specific requirements and provide any necessary documentation from the hospital to support your stay.

What happens if I have complications after I return home?

Our support doesn't end when you fly home. Upon discharge, you will receive a comprehensive medical report in English for your US doctor.
